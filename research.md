## What is Cloud Computing?
Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services such as servers, storage, databases, networking, and software over the internet. Instead of owning physical hardware, users can rent resources on demand. Cloud services are scalable, cost-efficient, and accessible from anywhere. Users typically pay only for what they use. Cloud computing enables faster deployment and easier management of applications.

## Cloud Provider Free Tier Comparison

### 1. Microsoft Azure
**Free Tier Includes:**
- $200 free credit
- Selected services free for 12 months
- Always-free services with limited usage

**Duration:**
- $200 credit valid for 30 days
- 12 months for selected free services

**Key Limitations:**
- Credit expires after 30 days
- Usage limits apply
- Credit card required for verification

---

### 2. Amazon Web Services (AWS)
**Free Tier Includes:**
- 750 hours/month of EC2 (t2.micro/t3.micro)
- 5 GB S3 storage
- Limited access to RDS, Lambda, DynamoDB

**Duration:**
- 12 months for most services
- Some services are always free with limits

**Key Limitations:**
- New users only
- Easy to accidentally incur charges
- Credit card required

---

### 3. Google Cloud Platform (GCP)
**Free Tier Includes:**
- $300 free credit
- Always-free Compute Engine (small VM)
- Free storage and BigQuery usage (limited)

**Duration:**
- $300 credit valid for 12 months

**Key Limitations:**
- Limited VM size
- Region restrictions
- Credit card required

---

### 4. DigitalOcean
**Free Tier Includes:**
- $200 free credit

**Duration:**
- Valid for 60 days

**Key Limitations:**
- No always-free tier
- Payment method required

---

### 5. Linode
**Free Tier Includes:**
- $100 free credit

**Duration:**
- Valid for approximately 60 days

**Key Limitations:**
- Limited trial duration
- Payment method required
